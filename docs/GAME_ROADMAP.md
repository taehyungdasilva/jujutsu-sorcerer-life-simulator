# GAME_ROADMAP.md

# Jujutsu Sorcerer Life Simulator

Version: Pre-Production v0.1

---

# Project Vision

Create the definitive Jujutsu Kaisen life simulation experience.

The player does not control a canon character.

The player creates an original sorcerer and lives an entire life within the Jujutsu world, making decisions that influence relationships, power, history, and future generations.

The experience should feel like:

* Persona's calendar and social systems
* Football Manager's statistics and records
* Crusader Kings' emergent storytelling
* Roguelite permadeath and replayability
* Jujutsu Kaisen's philosophy, danger, and power system

The objective is not simply:

"Become the strongest."

The objective is:

"Become the greatest sorcerer in history and leave a lasting legacy."

---

# Core Design Pillars

## Pillar 1: Sorcerer Life Simulation

The player should genuinely feel like a Jujutsu student and later a professional sorcerer.

The game must simulate:

* School life
* Training
* Missions
* Relationships
* Injuries
* Research
* Politics
* Survival
* Growth

Every day should create meaningful choices.

---

## Pillar 2: Meaningful Decisions

No activity should be purely cosmetic.

Every decision should have consequences.

Examples:

Studying:

* Knowledge

- Less social time

Taking difficult missions:

* Faster growth

- Higher death risk

Ignoring relationships:

* More training time

- Lose support and opportunities

The player cannot do everything.

Tradeoffs create strategy.

---

## Pillar 3: Lore Accuracy

The game must feel like it was made by passionate Jujutsu Kaisen fans.

Avoid:

* Generic RPG mechanics
* Generic elemental systems
* Random anime powers
* Power scaling that ignores canon

Systems should respect:

* Cursed Energy
* Techniques
* Domains
* Binding Vows
* Reverse Cursed Technique
* Clan politics
* Character philosophies

---

## Pillar 4: Emergent Stories

The game should generate memorable stories.

Examples:

"I sacrificed my arm to survive Shibuya."

"I became Gojo's favorite student."

"I died at 18 protecting civilians."

"I surpassed Nanami's Black Flash record."

"I discovered a Binding Vow used by future generations."

The game should create stories rather than scripted playthroughs.

---

## Pillar 5: Legacy

Death is permanent.

Every run should matter.

Dead characters become history.

Future runs can discover:

* Diaries
* Research papers
* Historical records
* Legendary weapons
* Famous Binding Vows
* Statues
* Titles

The world should remember previous characters.

---

# Long-Term Vision

The player creates:

Name

Lives:

School Life
→ Missions
→ Relationships
→ Growth
→ Major Incidents
→ Adulthood
→ Death

Leaves behind:

Records
→ Discoveries
→ Titles
→ History

# Development Philosophy

Simple presentation.

Deep systems.

Prioritize:

Menus
Statistics
Text
Choices
Simulation

Do not prioritize:

3D graphics
Complex animations
Action combat
Large open worlds
Multiplayer systems

Depth is more important than visuals.
